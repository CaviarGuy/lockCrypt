Modified Ceasar Cipher

A modified Caesar cipher is a variation of the Caesar cipher that produces ciphertext that is easier to read and harder to crack. It's a basic encryption technique that replaces each letter in the plaintext with a letter that's a certain number of places down the alphabet.

To encrypt a message proposed algorithm requires plaintext and encryption key. The encryption key is an integer value and it determines alphabet to be used for substitution. It is based on modulo twenty six arithmetic to ensure that integer value wraps round in case encryption key supplied is more than twenty six

When operating on 2 symbols at once, a Hill cipher offers no particular advantage over Playfair or the bifid cipher, and in fact is weaker than either, and slightly more laborious to operate by pencil-and-paper. As the dimension increases, the cipher rapidly becomes infeasible for a human to operate by hand.
